Connecting Slack & Gmail

Keep it simple: You do NOT need to create a Slack bot or app. Claude Code can send Slack messages using a simple webhook URL, and Gmail just works through your browser login. No coding required.

Slack — Using Webhooks (Easiest)

A webhook is just a special URL. When Claude sends a message to that URL, it shows up in your Slack channel. No bot setup, no app install, no permissions headaches.

One-time setup (done by your team lead):

  1. Go to slack.com/apps and search for "Incoming WebHooks"
  2. Click Add to Slack
  3. Pick the channel you want messages to go to (e.g., #reports)
  4. Click Add Incoming WebHooks Integration
  5. You'll see a Webhook URL that looks like:
    https://hooks.slack.com/services/REPLACE-WITH-YOUR-WEBHOOK-URL
  6. Copy this URL and save it somewhere your team can access it

That's it for setup. One person does this once. Everyone else just uses the URL.

How to use it in Claude Code:

When you're telling Claude what to do (in a scheduled workflow or regular conversation), just mention the webhook:

After generating the report, send a summary to Slack using this webhook URL:
https://hooks.slack.com/services/PASTE-YOUR-WEBHOOK-URL-HERE

Post it to the channel with a short summary of the results.

Claude will handle the rest — it knows how to format and send Slack webhook messages.

Example — Daily report to Slack:

Update the scheduled workflow so it does this every day at 9am:
- Check for any new issues or PRs from the last 24 hours
- Write a short summary
- Send the summary to Slack using this webhook:
  https://hooks.slack.com/services/PASTE-YOUR-WEBHOOK-URL-HERE

Keep it short and easy to read.
What do the messages look like?
They show up in your Slack channel as a regular message from "incoming-webhook" (you can customize the name and icon in the webhook settings). It's not from a bot or a person — just a simple notification.

Gmail — Send From Your Own Account

Claude Code can send emails as you (from your Gmail account). No extra setup needed — it uses the same Gmail connection as the Claude desktop app.

First-time setup:

  1. Open the Claude desktop app
  2. Ask Claude to send an email (just tell it in plain English)
  3. Claude will ask you to connect your Google account
  4. Click Allow in the browser popup
  5. Done — Claude can now send emails from your Gmail

That's it. You only need to do this once. After that, Claude can read and send emails whenever you ask.

How to use it:

Just tell Claude what to email and who to send it to:

Send an email to the team at team@company.com with a summary of today's report. Keep it brief and professional.

Or in a scheduled workflow:

Update the scheduled workflow so it does this every Monday at 9am:
- Generate a weekly summary of project activity
- Email it to team@company.com from my Gmail
- Subject line: "Weekly Project Summary"

Keep the email short — just the highlights.
Note: The email comes from YOUR Gmail account (your name, your email address). It's not from a bot or generic account. This means the team sees it as a normal email from you.
Troubleshooting

"Gmail not connected" → Open the Claude desktop app, ask it to "send a test email to myself", and it will walk you through connecting your Google account again.

"Permission denied" → When the Google popup appears, make sure you check ALL the permission boxes and click "Allow."

What NOT to Do

  • Don't create a Slack bot — webhooks are 10x easier and do the same thing for notifications
  • Don't share your Gmail password — Claude uses secure OAuth (the "Allow" button in your browser), never your password
  • Don't put webhook URLs in public repos — anyone with the URL can post to your Slack channel. Keep it in a private config or environment variable
